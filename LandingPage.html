    What is Manga?

    Manga are comics or graphic novels created in Japan or using the Japanese language, according to the style developed there in the late 19th century. Manga has a long and complex early history in early Japanese art. In Japanese, "manga" refers to all kinds of cartoons, comics, and animations. For English speakers, "manga" has the narrower meaning of "Japanese comics", in line with the use of "anime" inside and outside Japan. Manga is usually printed in a comic book format called tankōbon. A tankōbon usually contains about 100-200 pages. Manga may also be published in comic magazines called shōnen or shōjo. Comic magazines usually contain several different manga stories. Manga has a variety of genres, including action, comedy, fantasy, horror, mystery, romance, and science fiction. Manga can also be divided by target audience, such as shōnen (for boys), shōjo (for girls), seinen (for adult men), and josei (for adult women). Manga is one of the most popular art forms in Japan. It is also highly popular around the world. It has been translated into many languages and published in many countries.
